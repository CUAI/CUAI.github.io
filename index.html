Overview

Cornell University Vision and Learning (CUVL) is a club focused on undergraduate research and publication, as well as getting people involved with/up-to-date with current CV research.

The club is organized into the following three parts:

Reading Group (open to anyone, expected 6-15 people)
A reading group portion, where we discuss papers as well as general research - this is intended for more experienced people looking to discuss the state of the art. People from the Research group will be expected to attend in order to discuss potentially relevant research papers.

Research-focused (selected through applications, 6-8 people total, taken for graded research credit)
A small group of students that will work closely with the club’s officers, aiming to publish at top ML/CV conferences (CVPR, NIPS, etc.). People that already have some experience will likely be a good fit, as we would like to hit the ground running.

Open Class (open to anyone, expected 20+ people)
A class similar to Stanford’s CS231n - we will start with the basics and get up to the state-of-the-art in several subfields. See our slides from the Spring 2018 semester ACSU Reading Group for a preliminary look at the kind of content we hope to provide.
